## LANCache Manager is moving to the Functional Source License (FSL-1.1-MIT)

Hey everyone,

Starting with the next release, LANCache Manager is changing its license from
MIT to the **Functional Source License (FSL-1.1-MIT)**.

**Nothing changes for the people who actually use this project.** You can still:

- Self-host it, for free, for yourself, your LAN parties, your business, or your
  gaming cafe.
- Read the full source, fork it, and modify it for your own use.
- Get every future feature and fix.

**The one thing that's now off-limits:** taking this project, reskinning it, and
selling or hosting it as a competing product or service. That's the behavior
this change is meant to stop, and only that.

**It still becomes open source.** Every release automatically converts to the
MIT License two years after it ships. So the code keeps flowing back to the
community on a rolling basis, just with a delay that stops day-one resellers
from undercutting the project.

### Details

- Versions up to and including **1.10.3** were MIT and **stay MIT forever**.
  Nothing is being clawed back.
- The change applies to **new releases from here forward**.
- The project **name and logo** are covered by a separate
  [Trademark Policy](TRADEMARK.md) so forks can't pass themselves off as the
  official project.
- Full text: [LICENSE](LICENSE) · historical MIT notice: [LICENSE-MIT](LICENSE-MIT).

Why the change? To keep LANCache Manager healthy and sustainable for the long
run. The goal is simple: keep it free and fully usable for everyone who actually
runs it, while making sure the project can't just be repackaged and resold out
from under the community. The two-year conversion to MIT means the code keeps
flowing back to everyone over time. Thanks for being here, and happy to answer
any questions in the comments.

— Regix
