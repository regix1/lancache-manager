## LANCache Manager is moving to the GNU AGPL v3

Hey everyone,

Starting with the next release, LANCache Manager is changing its license from
MIT to the **GNU Affero General Public License v3.0 (AGPL-3.0)**.

To be clear up front: it stays **fully open source**. This is a stronger
open-source license, not a move away from open source.

**Nothing changes for the people who actually use this project.** You can still:

- Self-host it, for free, for yourself, your LAN parties, your business, or your
  gaming cafe.
- Read the full source, fork it, and modify it.
- Get every future feature and fix.

**What's new:** if you run a *modified* version for other people (including as a
hosted service), you have to make your modified source available to them under
the same license, with the original copyright intact. In other words,
improvements get shared back, and the project can't be quietly turned into a
closed, proprietary product.

### Details

- Versions up to and including **1.10.3** were MIT and stay MIT. Nothing is being
  clawed back.
- The change applies to **new releases from here forward**.
- The project **name and logo** are covered by a separate
  [Trademark Policy](TRADEMARK.md) so forks can't pass themselves off as the
  official project.
- Full text: [LICENSE](LICENSE).

Why the change? To keep LANCache Manager healthy and open for the long run, and
to make sure improvements flow back to the community instead of disappearing into
closed forks. Thanks for being here, and happy to answer any questions in discord.

— Regix
